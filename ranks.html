Mission Viejo Chess Club

The Mission Viejo Chess Club has Established a club ranking system for its members that will allow you to progress from pawn up to king by being active and attending club events either online, or in person, and showing chess knowledge. Below are the ranks and required knowledge for each rank.

Rank 1 - Pawn

The Pawn is the 1st rank of MVCC and is achieved by participating in a live online or in person event. Additionally, the member must demonstrate knowledge of how all pieces move. Any games played at that event will fulfill this requirement.

Rank 2 - Knight

To achieve the rank of Knight, the member must currently hold the rank of Pawn, attend another event, and show an understanding of the first four MVCC Opening Principles via demonstration, quiz, or verbal explanation.

Rank 3 - Bishop

To achieve the rank of Bishop, the member must currently how the rank of Knight, attend another event, and show an understanding of at least three of the MVCC Middlegame Principles. This can be met via demonstration, quiz, or verbal explanation.

Rank 4 - Rook

To achieve the rank of Rook, the member must currently how the rank of Bishop, attend another event, and show an understanding of two techniques from each of the following two areas (for a total of four skills). Areas are MVCC Endgame Principles and MVCC Mating Patterns.

Rank 5 - Queen

To achieve the rank of Queen, the member must currently how the rank of Rook, attend another event, and show an understanding of pins, forks, and discovered attacks from MVCC Key Chess Tactics.

Rank 6 - King

The rank of King is the highest rank in the club. To achieve this rank, the member must currently hold the rank of Queen, attend another event, and demonstrate that they have memorized the 17 moves of "The Opera Game", played by the Duke of Brunswick and Count Isouard, in 1858. This is considered one of the greatest chess games ever played. Paul Morphy was the greatest player of his time (pre-world championship). Morphy developed his pieces fast, gained initative, and attacked. Paul Morphy's games are perfect for our club to study, starting with the Opera Game.
